# Entry-Level Consultant Simulation: Operations Strategy Day at Aster Lane Advisory

## Job Profile

The player is an entry-level consultant at Aster Lane Advisory, a mid-to-large U.S. consulting firm. The role is modeled on management analysts who gather information, analyze business data, document findings, prepare recommendations, and communicate with managers and clients. [onet]

The day focuses on a retail operations strategy case for HarborMart, a regional grocery chain. This keeps the experience grounded in the entry-level consulting mix of spreadsheets, source workspaces, manager feedback, client calls, Slack updates, and slide drafting. ## Setting

Aster Lane is helping HarborMart understand why online pickup orders are late in some stores. The client has sent a messy store-level extract with pickup order volume, labor hours, late-pickup rate, inconsistent metric definitions, and several missing fields. [yt:consulting_client_call_notes]

The player is not the senior strategist. Their realistic job is to structure ambiguity, keep the analysis traceable, identify caveats, ask precise follow-up questions, and make a manager's review easier. [yt:consulting_day_notes] [reddit:consulting_feedback]

## Source-Derived Work Mix

The role is primarily digital artifact work and cognitive analysis: the consultant works through a case workspace, spreadsheet excerpts, metric definitions, a prioritization board, Slack updates, and slide notes. It also includes substantial written documentation and a realistic spoken client call, while physical/procedural work is minimal for this desk-based role. ## Day Arc

The morning starts with a manager Slack message, a client email, a small KPI extract, and a short runway before a 15-minute Marcus call. The student chooses the first move and sees a realistic consequence before moving into structured work. [yt:consulting_day_notes] [reddit:consulting_workload]

The first production task is a short set of hypothesis-question pairs for Marcus. The student must connect each early hypothesis to a specific client question rather than simply listing possible causes. [onet]

Next, the student triages messy inputs: inconsistent late-pickup definitions, missing labor records, a client executive asking for an answer today, an outlier store, manager-review timing, and store-manager interviews. The goal is to prioritize by urgency and decision impact. [onet] [yt:consulting_client_call_notes]

During the remote voice meeting with HarborMart's operations director, the student must state the early pattern, name the caveat, ask for missing definitions or data, and align on the next checkpoint without over-claiming. [yt:consulting_client_call_notes] [reddit:consulting_client_calls]

In the afternoon, the student turns the source data into three findings and builds a client-ready slide deck with speaker notes. These tasks emphasize that consulting output is not only calculation; it is turning partial evidence into a useful recommendation. [onet] [yt:consulting_slides_notes] [reddit:consulting_feedback]

## Learning Goals

Students should experience the junior consultant's core tension: move quickly, but protect credibility. They should learn to frame hypotheses, clean only the data that matters, keep caveats visible, and communicate uncertainty in answer-first language. [yt:consulting_day_notes] [yt:consulting_client_call_notes]

Students should also experience the production rhythm of consulting: source workspace to hypothesis-question pairs, pairs to triage, triage to client conversation, conversation to findings, and findings to slide deck. [onet] [yt:consulting_slides_notes] [reddit:consulting_excel_slides]

## Required Work Products

The simulation requires the player to choose a first move, write hypothesis-question pairs, place all triage items on a priority matrix, handle a spoken client call, write structured findings, and build a slide deck with speaker notes.
