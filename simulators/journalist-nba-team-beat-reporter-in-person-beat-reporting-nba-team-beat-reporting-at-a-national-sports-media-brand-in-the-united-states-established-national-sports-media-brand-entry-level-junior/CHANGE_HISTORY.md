## Human Review Feedback - 2026-06-30

Review decision: **Accepted with corrections**.

### Accepted Suggestions

- The quality-upgrader pass is accepted overall: voice-or-typed interview access, real learner-task progress labels, English final-report/rubric cleanup, article assembly usability, stale-route redirects, glossary/button interaction fixes, and Coach Harris medical-boundary QA all matched the intended Compassly quality direction.
- The single-article route and source-tray trimming are accepted because they reduce duplicate writing load while keeping the central beat-reporter judgment: use verified evidence, ask fair questions, and write one supported deadline article.
- The warmup and media scrum improvements are accepted because they keep task evidence close to where students act and carry earlier learner work into later interviews and writing.

### Accepted With Corrections

- The possession timeline design was useful, but its completion rule was not explicit enough for a high-school learner during live testing. The UI required at least 3 completed notes, where each completed note meant both a selected label and note text for the same moment, but the original instruction did not make that contract clear enough.
- The correction was to state the rule directly in the scenario instructions, reference panel, progress text, and locked-submit message: watch all moments, save at least 3 completed notes, and make clear that each completed note needs one label plus note text.
- This feedback should be treated as a general simulator-quality lesson, not only an NBA journalist fix.

### Rejected Suggestions

- None explicitly rejected in this review. The user correction does not reject the possession timeline task; it rejects leaving any scenario's completion requirements implicit or discoverable only through a disabled button.

### Reusable Quality Rule

Every required scenario must tell high-school students, near the action itself:

- what they need to do;
- how many items or sections are required;
- what counts as a completed item;
- where the evidence should be entered;
- why Next or Submit is still locked when they are not done.

If the submit gate checks structured evidence such as `label + note text`, the visible task copy, progress text, and blocker message must all use the same plain-language contract. This maps especially to checklist items 11, 17, 33, and 35.
