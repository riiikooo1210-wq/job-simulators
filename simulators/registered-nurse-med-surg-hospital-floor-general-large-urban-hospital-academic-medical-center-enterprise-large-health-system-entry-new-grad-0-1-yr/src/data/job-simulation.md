# Registered Nurse — Metropolitan University Health System Simulation

## Company Profile
- **Name:** Metropolitan University Health System (MUHS)
- **Industry:** Healthcare, Academic Medical Center
- **Size:** 15,000+ employees, 800-bed main campus
- **Product/Service:** MUHS is a leading urban teaching hospital providing comprehensive patient care, from routine procedures to level 1 trauma and complex specialty services. Its mission is to provide excellent patient care while training the next generation of healthcare professionals.
- **Why this company hires people in this role:** Registered Nurses are the backbone of patient care at MUHS. They are responsible for assessing patients, administering treatments, coordinating with the multidisciplinary team, and ensuring patient safety and positive outcomes on inpatient units `[onet]`.
- **The team the student is on:** You are on 5 North, a 32-bed general Medical-Surgical (Med-Surg) unit. The patient population is diverse, including post-operative patients, individuals with infections like cellulitis or pneumonia, and those with chronic illnesses requiring hospitalization.

## The Protagonist (You)
- **Role:** Registered Nurse, Level I (New Graduate)
- **Tenure:** You are 4 months into your first nursing job after graduating and passing your NCLEX. You are off orientation but still learning every day.
- **Reporting Line:** You report to the Charge Nurse on duty. Your key collaborators are other RNs, Patient Care Technicians (PCTs), physicians (attendings and residents), physical therapists, and case managers `[onet]`.

## The Day / Week

### Scene 1: Morning Handoff & Prioritization
**Setting and time:** Nurses' Station, 07:15
**What's happening and why:** The night nurse gives you a quick report on your four patients:
1.  **Room 512:** Mrs. Vance, 88F, post-op day 2 for a hip fracture. Vitals stable, but she was moaning in pain most of the night. The doctor has ordered IV Tylenol and oral ibuprofen only, per the daughter's request.
2.  **Room 514:** Mr. Jones, 65M, admitted yesterday for cellulitis of the left leg. On IV antibiotics. Stable.
3.  **Room 515:** Ms. Chen, 45F, post-op day 1 from an appendectomy. Tolerating a clear liquid diet, ambulating with assistance. She is awaiting discharge paperwork and education.
4.  **Room 518:** Mr. Henderson, 72M, admitted for community-acquired pneumonia. On oxygen via nasal cannula. Stable.

You need to decide how to structure the start of your day. Organizing and prioritizing your work is a critical nursing skill `[onet]`.
**What decision, deliverable, conversation, or hands-on action the student faces:** Based on the report, what is your first priority?
**Interaction mode:** Multiple-choice question

Which action will you take first?
A. Start the discharge paperwork and education for Ms. Chen in 515 to open up a bed.
B. Go to Room 512 to perform a full head-to-toe assessment on Mrs. Vance and address her pain.
C. Quickly lay eyes on all four of your patients to ensure everyone is safe and stable before starting assessments.
D. Begin pulling all 09:00 medications from the Omnicell to get ahead of the morning med pass `[yt:WZM7r4-psTY]`.

**What "good" looks like vs. "poor" looks like:**
*   **Good:** Choosing B or C. B focuses on the most acute and unstable issue (uncontrolled pain) `[onet]`. C is a valid safety check to quickly assess the whole assignment. Both demonstrate sound clinical judgment `[yt:KCMp8nm17no]`.
*   **Poor:** Choosing A or D. A is important but not urgent; the patient is stable. D is a task-oriented approach that dangerously skips the "assess before you medicate" rule of nursing `[onet]`.

**What's happening and why:** You enter the room to assess Mrs. Vance. She is grimacing and her vitals are elevated: HR 115, BP 168/92. She rates her pain around "8 to 9 out of 10." **What decision, deliverable, conversation, or hands-on action the student faces:** You need to speak with Sarah, using the clinical prep you just reviewed to explain the importance of pain management while respecting her concerns.
**Interaction mode:** AI-driven voice meeting

Your mother is in pain, but you are terrified of her becoming addicted to opioids because "addiction runs in the family" [reddit:1qyduhb]. You are firm that she should only receive ibuprofen. Your goal is to resist the nurse's attempts to provide stronger pain medication. Listen to the nurse's rationale. **What "good" looks like vs. "poor" looks like:**

## Glossary
- **Med-Surg:** Medical-Surgical, a hospital unit that cares for adult patients with a wide variety of illnesses and those recovering from surgery.
- **SBAR:** A structured communication tool for healthcare professionals: Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation.
- **EMR:** Electronic Medical Record; the digital version of a patient's chart.
- **STAT:** A medical term derived from the Latin *statim*, meaning immediately or urgently.
- **Pulse Oximeter:** A medical device that measures the oxygen saturation level in the blood.
- **Rapid Response Team (RRT):** A dedicated team of critical care providers that can be called to the bedside to assist with a patient who is rapidly declining.
- **Handoff:** The process of transferring patient information and responsibility from one healthcare provider to another, typically at the end of a shift.
- **Omnicell:** An automated medication dispensing cabinet used in hospitals to securely store and track medications.
