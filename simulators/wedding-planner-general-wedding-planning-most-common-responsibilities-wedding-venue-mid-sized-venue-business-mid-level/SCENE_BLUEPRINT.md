## Work Mix

- Summary: For a mid-level wedding planner inside a wedding venue, the source-derived job mix is dominated by timeline/vendor artifact work, client and vendor communication, and judgment under constraints, with meaningful but not dominant physical venue walkthrough work. O*NET is only a broad event-planning proxy; the simulator weights selected venue-side web/blog sources, WeddingWire's responsibility breakdown, and wedding-specific Reddit signals about timelines, vendor coordination, setup, room flips, rain plans, and venue-scope boundaries much more heavily.
- `physicalProceduralTool`: secondary ‚Äî Include a physical ballroom setup playground where the player manipulates visible objects and documents observations, rather than narrating setup as a completed task.
- `digitalToolArtifactWork`: major ‚Äî Make the player read a final details workspace, audit a flawed timeline, and compose a vendor alignment update in realistic venue work surfaces.
- `cognitiveAnalysisDecision`: major ‚Äî Use triage, timeline repair, and meeting decisions that require the player to identify dependencies instead of choosing generic reassuring answers.
- `writtenDocumentationArtifact`: major ‚Äî Require typed artifacts: a timeline audit, client meeting prep, setup note, and vendor alignment message.
- `spokenInterpersonalCommunication`: major ‚Äî Include an in-person voice meeting with the couple where venue laptop document notes stay visible and the player must clarify rain-plan scope without overpromising.
- `passiveMonitoringWaitingContextSwitching`: secondary ‚Äî Represent interruptions through the final-details workspace and vendor alignment scene, while keeping the playable work active and artifact-based.
