# Wedding Planner Simulation

## Job Profile

- Title: Wedding Planner
- Industry / sector: Wedding venue
- Company stage: Mid-sized venue business
- Seniority: Mid-level
- Subspecialty: General wedding planning / most common responsibilities
- Source note: O*NET does not have a dedicated wedding planner occupation, so the O*NET source is used only as a broad event-planning proxy. The role-specific design is driven primarily by wedding planning web/blog sources, Reddit wedding-planning discussions, and selected YouTube descriptions. [onet][src:easyweddings][src:ribbyhall][reddit:18j71e3]

## Fictional Workplace

The simulator takes place at **Willow & Stone Events**, a mid-sized wedding venue with a garden ceremony lawn, a ballroom, a small tasting room, and an in-house operations team. The player is a mid-level venue wedding planner assigned to the Rivera-Chen wedding, a 120-guest Saturday event with an outdoor ceremony, indoor reception, external florist and DJ, venue catering, and a weather-risk backup plan. [src:easyweddings][src:ribbyhall][reddit:u5p4sd]

At this venue, the planner is not a full-service independent planner for every personal detail, but the couple still expects the planner to protect the event flow, coordinate vendors, translate venue constraints, and keep them from being pulled into logistics on wedding day. That boundary is a recurring source of confusion in wedding-planning discussions, and WeddingWire's responsibility breakdown reinforces that planners handle many vendor, timeline, floor-plan, and day-of logistics while couples still own personal decisions and approvals. [src:weddingwire][reddit:1rvsqof][reddit:1syxiax]

## Work Reality

The most authentic work is a mix of desk planning, operational walkthroughs, client communication, vendor coordination, and rapid judgment under time pressure. The player should not simply choose generic options; they should read a final-details workspace, repair a flawed timeline, physically inspect and adjust a ballroom setup, briefly review an existing walkthrough document, speak with the couple in person, and send vendor DM replies. The selected web sources consistently describe coordinator days as both hands-on venue readiness and information-heavy coordination. [src:easyweddings][src:ribbyhall][src:firmlyrooted]

Wedding-day timelines are central artifacts. They connect ceremony start, reception start, dinner service, speeches, first dance, cake, vendor arrivals, room flips, family photos, and contingency triggers. A weak timeline with wrong times or unresolved TBDs creates downstream problems for the photographer, DJ, catering team, venue staff, and client confidence; planner-day sources also frame the day as beginning with timeline review before onsite work. [src:blovedboston][src:weddingwire][reddit:1oq2bzu]

Venue wedding planners also do visible procedural work. They walk rooms, compare setup against the BEO or final details sheet, check signage and guest-flow surfaces, verify that vendor staging does not block service paths, and correct issues before guests arrive. Ribby Hall's venue-side day shows ceremony-room dressing, placement of personal details, guest seating, processional support, and reception-room finishing touches, so the simulator includes a physical playground for ballroom setup rather than treating setup as narration. [src:ribbyhall][src:easyweddings][reddit:1cdubnv]

Client care is not soft fluff; it is part of the job deliverable. Venue-coordinator sources describe greeting, availability, emotional support, and calm handoff work alongside logistics, while Reddit discussions show that couples value not being interrupted by operational questions. The simulator includes an in-person voice meeting where the player must acknowledge stress, clarify scope, and steer the couple through rain-plan and vendor concerns. [src:easyweddings][src:ribbyhall][reddit:1kfjw3p]

The job also requires boundary management. Venue planners must work with external planners and vendors, explain venue policies, protect privacy and consent, and avoid promising services that belong to the client-side planner or an outside vendor. WeddingWire's responsibility framing helps separate planner/vendor coordination from couple-owned decisions, while Reddit discussions show conflict around venue-provided planners, banned coordinators, content requests, and unclear service packages. [src:weddingwire][reddit:u5p4sd][reddit:1ch49mf]

## Work-Mix Design

- **Digital tool / artifact work: major.** The player reads a final details workspace, audits a timeline, references vendor notes, and writes vendor DMs in venue-style tools. [src:firmlyrooted][src:weddingwire][reddit:1m4jjhw]
- **Written documentation / artifact production: major.** The player produces a timeline repair note, a setup note, and vendor Slack-style DM replies, while using an existing walkthrough document as a live reference rather than drafting a new meeting agenda. [src:blovedboston][src:weddingwire][reddit:1oq2bzu]
- **Spoken interpersonal communication: major.** The job regularly involves client meetings, supplier/vendor briefing, rehearsal conversations, guest-facing calm, and expectation gaps, so the simulator includes an in-person voice meeting. [src:easyweddings][src:ribbyhall][reddit:1kfjw3p]
- **Cognitive analysis / decision-making: major.** The player prioritizes a messy final-week situation, identifies timeline risks, chooses what to escalate, and balances venue policy with client care. [onet][reddit:1pf9dum]
- **Physical / procedural / tool manipulation: secondary but required.** Venue planners do not spend the entire day moving objects, but venue-side sources describe dressing ceremony rooms, placing personal touches, checking reception-room details, and coordinating physical handoffs. The simulator includes a ballroom setup playground with visible objects and drop targets. [src:ribbyhall][src:easyweddings][reddit:1o77ow0]
- **Passive monitoring / waiting / context switching: secondary.** The role includes interruptions and waiting for vendor replies, but passive time is represented as context pressure, not as a playable waiting scene. [reddit:1p6lccx][yt:XWD84tnkZ4Q]

## Simulator Arc

### Section 1: Final Details Triage

The player starts with a final-details workspace for the Rivera-Chen wedding. The source workspace includes a draft timeline with defects, a vendor note from the florist, a venue rain-plan policy, catering service timing, a DJ cue list, and a client note asking whether the outdoor ceremony can still happen. [src:firmlyrooted][src:weddingwire][reddit:1svzrcp]

The first playable moment is a bounded triage decision: what should the planner do first with 90 minutes before the final walkthrough? Each choice routes to a realistic immediate consequence before converging. A timeline-first choice gives the cleanest path; a decor-first, client-comfort-first, or escalation-first choice receives corrective context about dependencies. [reddit:1oq2bzu][reddit:1tkj9ry]

The first constructed-response task asks the player to audit a flawed timeline by commenting on five issues / open items. They must identify concrete conflicts, unresolved decisions, and timing risks before proposing corrected timing, sequencing, or confirmations. This is intentionally based on Reddit examples where incorrect times, TBD fields, and incomplete coordination can damage trust and execution. [reddit:1oq2bzu][reddit:1svzrcp]

### Section 2: Walkthrough and Client Meeting

The second section moves from desk work to venue work. In the ballroom setup playground, the player sees a 16:9 ballroom image with a sweetheart table, DJ booth, cake table, welcome table, gift/card box, guest book, escort-card display, service aisle, and florist staging bins. They must read the final setup sheet, place the card box and guest book on the welcome table, flag florist bins blocking the service path and ask the florist to move them to the vendor corner, mark the missing reserved-family signs, and write a concise setup note. [src:ribbyhall][src:easyweddings][reddit:1cdubnv]

After the ballroom walkthrough, the player reviews an already-open venue laptop document with the few facts they need to carry into the couple conversation. The document remains visible during the in-person voice meeting. The meeting tests whether the planner can validate stress without overpromising, explain the rain-plan trigger, clarify what the venue team will handle, and ask for missing decisions such as processional order, family photo contacts, and vendor arrival constraints. [src:ribbyhall][src:weddingwire][reddit:1pf9dum]

### Section 3: Vendor Alignment and Closeout

The final active task is a pair of vendor DM replies. A florist staging request, DJ cue concern, and rain-plan deadline must be turned into calm, vendor-specific operational replies. The player must be specific enough for each vendor to act, but not write a vague "please be flexible" note. [src:weddingwire][src:firmlyrooted][reddit:16csxp9]

The work period ends on a required assessment gate page. Only the button labeled exactly **View Assessment** routes to grading. No work scene routes directly into grading. [reddit:1cdubnv]

## Fictional Characters

- **Maya Patel**, Venue Operations Manager. Direct, practical, protective of staff capacity and venue policy. She expects the player to distinguish venue responsibilities from client-side planning work. [src:easyweddings][reddit:u5p4sd][reddit:1ch49mf]
- **Elena Rivera**, bride/client. Warm but anxious, worried that rain will make the event feel less special and that family members will be pulled into logistics. [src:ribbyhall][reddit:1kfjw3p][reddit:1p6lccx]
- **Marcus Chen**, groom/client. Detail-oriented, mostly concerned about guest flow, timing, and whether vendors have the latest information. [reddit:1svzrcp][reddit:1tkj9ry]
- **Jordan Ellis**, external florist lead. Talented but running tight because of delivery timing and staging constraints. [src:ribbyhall][reddit:16csxp9][reddit:1o77ow0]
- **Tessa Morgan**, DJ. Needs precise cue timing, processional names, speeches, and weather-plan timing so music and announcements do not drift. [src:weddingwire][reddit:1svzrcp][reddit:1o77ow0]

## Assessment Rubric Intent

The assessment should reward evidence-based planning, operational specificity, client communication, vendor coordination, and venue-scope judgment. It should penalize vague reassurance, ungrounded promises, missing dependencies, and generic "day-of magic" language that does not produce usable artifacts. [src:weddingwire][src:blovedboston][reddit:1oq2bzu]
