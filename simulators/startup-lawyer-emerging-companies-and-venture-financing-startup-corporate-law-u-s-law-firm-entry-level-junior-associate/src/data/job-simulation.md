# Entry-Level Startup Lawyer Simulation: Seed Financing Day at Northstar Legal

## Job Profile

The player is an entry-level junior associate at Northstar Legal, a fictional U.S. law firm with an emerging companies and venture capital practice. The role is modeled on lawyers who advise clients on legal transactions, interpret laws and regulations, prepare legal documents, gather information, negotiate agreements, and communicate with clients and colleagues in writing and calls. [onet]

The day is specifically a startup corporate law day, not litigation and not in-house counsel. The player supports a senior associate on a fictional Series Seed financing for LumenLoop, a startup client that wants to close quickly while investors are asking for clean corporate records, cap table consistency, board approvals, and a focused closing-checklist update. [yt:startup_practice_notes] [reddit:junior_detail_work]

## Setting

Northstar Legal represents LumenLoop, a fictional Delaware C-corporation raising a $2.6 million seed round from Meadowline Ventures. The partner wants the team to keep the deal moving, the senior associate wants clean issue spotting, and the founder wants speed without understanding why small corporate-record issues can slow a closing. [yt:startup_practice_notes] [reddit:tight_deadlines]

The realistic pressure is not that a first-year lawyer gives final legal advice alone. The realistic pressure is that the junior associate must read carefully, maintain the closing checklist, spot inconsistencies, draft concise questions, prepare client-ready but supervised communications, and escalate the right issues before senior review. [onet] [reddit:ask_better_questions]

## Day Arc

The morning begins with a senior associate's email, a data room, a draft closing checklist, a messy cap table export, and a target to identify open issues before the client call. The player must choose whether to polish the closing checklist, immediately email the client, start with cap table math, or map the documents against the closing conditions first. [onet] [reddit:attention_to_detail]

The first work product is a structured closing checklist update. The player must identify missing or risky items across corporate approvals, investor documents, cap table records, and signature logistics. This reflects junior corporate work: organizing information, checking document status, and making senior review easier. [onet] [reddit:junior_detail_work]

Midday, the player triages a set of deal signals: a founder says a SAFE note was "probably converted," a board consent lacks one director signature, the investor requests an IP assignment confirmation, a cap table line does not match the charter, the partner wants an update, and the company asks whether it can announce the round today. The task is to sort what affects closing, what affects client communication, and what can wait. [yt:junior_associate_notes] [reddit:diligence_and_summaries]

The player then joins a spoken founder call with the senior associate listening in. The founder wants to know why the deal cannot simply close today. The player must explain the issue in plain business language, ask for specific missing facts, take notes on confirmed facts for the post-call checklist comments, avoid overclaiming, protect confidentiality and accuracy, and defer final legal advice to senior review where appropriate. [onet] [yt:lawyer_occupation_notes]

In the afternoon, the player annotates the saved closing checklist with call notes, status changes, remaining needs, and Maya review points. This is a realistic junior legal work product: an internal checklist that turns messy call facts into something a senior associate can review and act on quickly. [reddit:ask_better_questions] [reddit:attention_to_detail]

## Learning Goals

Students should feel the entry-level startup lawyer's mix of legal precision, client pressure, and document production: every date, defined term, signature block, entity name, and cap table number can matter, but not every issue deserves equal urgency. [onet] [reddit:attention_to_detail]

Students should also experience the communication craft of junior legal work: ask grouped questions, name the legal or closing consequence, write in a way a senior lawyer can review quickly, and avoid giving casual answers before the facts and supervising lawyer are aligned. [yt:lawyer_occupation_notes] [reddit:ask_better_questions]

## Required Work Products

The simulation requires the player to choose a first move, update a closing checklist, prepare for and handle a spoken founder call with notes, and annotate the checklist after the call. This keeps the simulator centered on actual startup-law artifacts rather than legal trivia. [onet] [yt:startup_practice_notes] [reddit:junior_detail_work]
