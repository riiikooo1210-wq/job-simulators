# Job Simulation: Public Relations Specialist

## Job Profile

- Title: Public Relations Specialist
- Industry: Large food and beverage brand / consumer packaged goods
- Company stage: Enterprise
- Seniority: Entry / junior
- Subspecialty: General public relations, media relations, and brand communications
- Simulator language: English

## Source-Derived Job Reality

Public relations specialists promote and protect an organization's public image, write or select material for release to media, respond to media information requests, maintain external relationships, post and update web or social content, and advise internal teams on public-facing communication choices [onet]. O*NET also shows that PR specialists use email, phone, face-to-face or team discussion, writing, decision-making, time pressure, and computer-based work every day, which makes this a desk-work simulator centered on artifacts, communication, and judgment rather than physical procedures [onet].

The collected day-in-the-life videos repeatedly describe PR work as a mix of email, planning, reports, press releases, media alerts, media lists, pitching reporters, coverage tracking, social monitoring, and client or internal coordination [yt:CL7FiNHYe7E][yt:E4frjGmiIiE]. The broader PR explainer video emphasizes that PR differs from advertising because it is largely earned media, and that journalist fit matters because irrelevant pitches damage relationships [yt:Ys4vsMjD8s4].

Reddit practitioner discussions add a useful realism layer: PR is not just press releases, media relations is a core but narrower subset of PR, and junior people often support media lists, pitches, monitoring, reporting, press-release drafting, and escalation rather than owning the whole strategy alone [reddit:cbwkbr][reddit:1eb8l3u][reddit:13swl3u]. Reddit threads also highlight the difference between press-release distribution and media pitching, the practical use of tools such as Cision, Meltwater, and Muck Rack, and the need to triage crisis or issues signals before overreacting [reddit:1smr65l][reddit:1jkfvz9][reddit:1kwwplo].

## Work-Mix Design

The active work mix should be mostly digital artifact work, writing, analysis, and interpersonal communication. Physical/procedural/tool manipulation is rare for this role because the real job is mainly email, documents, media databases, Slack-style coordination, monitoring dashboards, and calls [onet][yt:E4frjGmiIiE][reddit:13swl3u].

- Digital tool / artifact work: dominant. The student should use source workspaces, media-list cards, email compose windows, monitoring queues, and structured templates [onet][reddit:1jkfvz9].
- Written documentation / artifact production: dominant. The student should draft reporter angles, a pitch email, and a short external correction request or holding statement [onet][yt:CL7FiNHYe7E][reddit:k444le].
- Cognitive analysis / decision-making: major. The student should decide which media contacts fit, which issue signals are urgent, and how much to escalate [onet][reddit:1rdy6ij][reddit:1kwwplo].
- Spoken / interpersonal communication: major. The student should speak with a skeptical journalist and manage relationship tone while protecting the brand's credibility [onet][yt:Ys4vsMjD8s4][reddit:1ic95si].
- Passive monitoring / context switching: secondary. Monitoring matters, but the simulator should make the student act on signals instead of simply watching dashboards [onet][yt:E4frjGmiIiE][reddit:p0tife].
- Physical / procedural / tool manipulation: rare. No physical playground is needed because the role's concrete tools are digital work surfaces [onet].

## Fictional Company and Scenario

The simulator takes place at Northstar Foods, a fictional enterprise food and beverage company. The student is a junior Public Relations Specialist on the Brand Communications team. It is the morning of a launch for "Luma Fizz," a low-sugar sparkling fruit tea sold through grocery and convenience channels. The launch is exciting but sensitive: the product uses a natural sweetener blend, is produced in a facility that also handles tree nuts, and has already prompted a few social questions about ingredients and transparency.

The student's job is not to own the entire launch strategy. A junior PR specialist should prepare accurate source-based materials, help target the right journalists, draft concise media outreach, speak professionally with a reporter, monitor issue signals, and escalate with a clear recommendation when a reputational risk appears [onet][reddit:13swl3u][reddit:1p5tvlm].

## Fictional Cast

- Maya Chen, Senior PR Manager. Calm, specific, and protective of reporter trust. She expects the student to be useful, accurate, and concise.
- Jordan Reyes, Brand Marketing Lead. Enthusiastic about Luma Fizz and sometimes tempted to overclaim the product benefits.
- Lena Ortiz, Food and beverage journalist at The Daily Table. Skeptical, time-pressed, and interested in facts that matter to readers.
- Priya Nair, Quality and Regulatory Liaison. Precise about ingredient language, allergen statements, and what can or cannot be said publicly.

## Active Scene Plan

1. Morning launch workspace. The student receives the launch brief, approved facts, reporter angles, and risk notes before making any public-facing decisions [onet][reddit:1kwwplo].
2. First move Slack choice. The student chooses how to start the morning. Each option creates a realistic consequence before converging [yt:CL7FiNHYe7E][reddit:13swl3u].
3. Media target table. The student builds a focused media list from visible journalist cards, explaining why each reporter fits and what angle to use [yt:Ys4vsMjD8s4][reddit:1smr65l].
4. Reporter pitch email. The student writes a concise, source-grounded pitch to a food and beverage journalist [onet][reddit:1eabvxy].
5. Live journalist call. The student joins a remote voice meeting with Lena, answers questions, avoids overclaiming, and offers the right follow-up [onet][reddit:1ic95si].
6. Monitoring queue triage. The student sorts issue signals by urgency and explains the top priority [yt:E4frjGmiIiE][reddit:p0tife].
7. Blog correction request. The student writes a calm external correction email to a wellness blog that used unapproved clean/detox language, using confirmed facts and approved replacement wording without threatening the outlet [reddit:1kwwplo][reddit:1p5tvlm][reddit:k444le].
8. Assessment gate. The simulated day is complete; the user clicks exactly "View Assessment" before grading begins.

## Rubric Emphasis

The assessment should reward evidence use, media targeting judgment, professional writing, reporter relationship management, and issue-response discipline. Strong work is specific, sourced, transparent, and audience-aware. Weak work blasts generic pitches, hides uncertainty, overclaims product benefits, treats press-release distribution as earned coverage, or requests corrections in a threatening or promotional way [onet][reddit:1smr65l][reddit:1rdy6ij][reddit:1kwwplo].
