# Urban Planner Simulation - Phase 2 Story Document

## Job profile

- Title: Urban Planner [onet]
- Setting: Large private consulting / engineering firm supporting a private urban development client [reddit:j0fpgs][reddit:197qj02]
- Seniority: Entry-level / junior [reddit:10sbwur]
- Subspecialty: General urban planning, centered on land use, zoning, development review support, community engagement, and GIS-supported planning [onet]

## Source-derived role reality

Urban and regional planners develop plans and programs for land use and physical facilities, advise on feasibility and regulatory conformance, prepare narrative and graphic reports, review proposals, conduct research, maintain maps and databases, respond to public inquiries, and coordinate with public officials, developers, engineers, and community members [onet].

The sources consistently distinguish planning from freeform city design. Planners often work as analysts and advisors: they compare a proposal to adopted plans, codes, zoning, environmental constraints, transportation assumptions, and community concerns, then synthesize the findings for decision-makers or clients [yt:Ew6MAOUhRfI][reddit:16d1cn6][reddit:195iijq].

For this private consulting version, the day should feel client- and deadline-driven. The junior planner supports a project manager by checking entitlement constraints, organizing agency comments, preparing meeting questions, and drafting post-call response language for the project team. Reddit discussions of private-sector planning emphasize zoning research, entitlement packages, municipal comment responses, client communication, and cross-disciplinary coordination [reddit:j0fpgs][reddit:197qj02].

Most active work is desk-based: email, document review, plan review, GIS/map interpretation, reports, spreadsheets, meetings, and public or stakeholder communication. O*NET reports daily email, frequent face-to-face/team discussion, telephone conversations, indoor controlled work, written memos, public contact, and work with computers [onet]. Day-in-the-life sources describe opening email, responding to applicants or public inquiries, pre-application meetings, project review, coordinating with engineering/fire/water/public works, and preparing for planning commission or council questions [yt:Ew6MAOUhRfI][yt:L6f_MYAelMg][yt:O81xcWGNSnc].

The simulator should therefore avoid a physical construction-site fantasy. A small amount of site context can appear as map/photo evidence, but the player's real work should happen through concrete digital artifacts: a client brief, zoning excerpt, parcel map, comment log, constraints scan, meeting-prep note, voice meeting, and pre-app follow-up tracker [onet][reddit:valasv][reddit:xpy1u1].

## Fictional scenario

The player is a junior planner at VerdantWorks, a fictional national planning, engineering, and environmental consulting firm. The client, Harbor & Pine Development, is evaluating a mixed-use redevelopment concept called Riverbend Yard on an underused industrial parcel near a commuter rail stop.

The project manager, Priya Nair, needs the junior planner to produce a first-pass feasibility workspace before a 3:30 PM pre-application call with the City of Eastbank planning staff. The client wants optimism; the city source record shows constraints. The player's job is to be useful without overclaiming: identify what the record supports, what needs follow-up, what could affect entitlement risk, and how to communicate tradeoffs clearly.

## Core workplace artifacts

- Client brief: Harbor & Pine wants 220 apartments, 18,000 square feet of ground-floor retail, a public riverwalk segment, and reduced parking because of transit access [onet][reddit:j0fpgs].
- Site facts: 6.8-acre former warehouse parcel, adjacent to a low-rise residential neighborhood, near Eastbank Station, partly inside a mapped flood overlay, and abutting a riverfront habitat buffer.
- Zoning excerpt: existing I-MU Industrial Mixed Use district; residential allowed only with conditional use permit; height limit 55 feet by right, 75 feet with public-benefit bonus; minimum active-frontage requirement on Main Street; parking reductions possible with transportation demand management.
- City comment log: planning staff requests a conditional-use narrative, traffic memo, stormwater/floodplain review, affordable housing discussion, and early neighborhood meeting.
- Community input sample: residents support riverwalk access and cleanup but worry about traffic, building height, displacement pressure, and whether public space will feel private.
- Internal constraints: civil engineering needs the floodplain assumptions; transportation needs a trip-generation starting point; the client wants a readable risk summary by end of day.

## Work mix design

Physical/procedural/tool manipulation is rare. The job includes occasional site visits and visual inspection, but for this entry-level consulting day, the active work is office-based and digital [onet][yt:Ew6MAOUhRfI][reddit:xpy1u1].

Digital tool and artifact work is major. The player reads a planning workspace, map-style information, zoning text, a comment log, and message threads, then works inside document, meeting-prep, voice-call, and follow-up tracker surfaces [onet][reddit:j0fpgs][reddit:197qj02].

Cognitive analysis and decision-making are dominant. The central challenge is not choosing a favorite development concept; it is translating incomplete regulatory, community, and technical evidence into defensible planning judgment [onet][reddit:16d1cn6][reddit:195iijq].

Written documentation is major. The player drafts a constraints scan, meeting-prep questions, and a pre-app follow-up tracker, which mirrors O*NET's emphasis on reports, memos, maps/data, documentation, and written communication [onet][reddit:10sbwur][reddit:q8z5y1].

Spoken and interpersonal communication is secondary but important. The player must conduct a spoken pre-application call with a city planner, using prepared notes while staying neutral and specific [onet][yt:Ew6MAOUhRfI][reddit:wjjh8p].

Passive monitoring and context switching are secondary. The day includes incoming Slack and shifting deadlines, but the simulator should grade active work products rather than waiting [reddit:1pgbk0r][reddit:xpy1u1].

## Proposed scene arc

1. **Welcome to VerdantWorks.** The player enters a consulting office desk with a planning workspace, project dashboard, and schedule [onet][reddit:197qj02].
2. **Riverbend Yard packet.** The player reviews the client brief, zoning excerpt, map facts, city comment log, and community input.
3. **First move Slack choice.** Priya asks how the player will spend the first hour. Choices route to distinct consequences before converging.
4. **Constraints scan.** The player creates a structured planning note: opportunity, constraints, missing information, and next questions.
5. **Prepare for the pre-application call.** The player drafts non-leading questions for city staff based on the source workspace and constraints scan.
6. **City planner voice meeting.** The player conducts a spoken remote call with an Eastbank planning staff member, using their prep note.
7. **Pre-app follow-up tracker.** The player uses the call notes and source workspace to write follow-up tracker rows that capture staff guidance, team revisions, owners, and open questions.
8. **Day complete / View Assessment.** A gate page confirms the planning day is complete before grading.

## Grading priorities

The assessment should reward evidence-based planning judgment, regulatory and entitlement issue spotting, community/stakeholder awareness, professional communication, and clear written work. It should penalize unsupported certainty, ignoring code or environmental constraints, treating planners as final decision-makers, overpromising approvals, or writing vague client updates without next actions [onet][yt:Ew6MAOUhRfI][reddit:195iijq][reddit:10sbwur].

## Scene design requirements

Every major scene should show the source material before asking the student to act. The source workspace must remain visible or summarized in later work. Prep notes from the call-prep scene must be visible in the voice meeting. The pre-app follow-up tracker must reference prior analysis and the city call notes. Multiple-choice must not dominate the simulator; it is limited to the first-move decision with consequence redirects [reddit:1pgbk0r][reddit:q8z5y1].
