## Work Mix

- Summary: The collected sources show entry-level urban planning in a large private consulting and engineering firm as a desk-centered, artifact-heavy role. The junior planner reads zoning and site workspaces, checks regulatory and environmental constraints, prepares maps or map-based summaries, coordinates with engineers and city staff, supports pre-application meetings, and writes client-ready notes. The work is not freeform city design; it is evidence-based planning judgment under legal, technical, community, and client constraints.
- `physicalProceduralTool`: rare — No physical_playground is needed for this junior consulting day. Site reality appears through map and parcel artifacts rather than hand manipulation.
- `digitalToolArtifactWork`: major — Most active scenes occur inside realistic planning workspace, document/prep, voice-call, and follow-up tracker surfaces.
- `cognitiveAnalysisDecision`: dominant — The central graded tasks require issue spotting, uncertainty handling, entitlement-risk prioritization, and defensible recommendations.
- `writtenDocumentationArtifact`: major — The player must draft a constraints scan, call-prep questions, and a pre-app follow-up tracker.
- `spokenInterpersonalCommunication`: secondary — Include a remote voice meeting with a city planner because the private consultant must ask targeted questions and communicate uncertainty professionally.
- `passiveMonitoringWaitingContextSwitching`: secondary — Use incoming Slack, a same-day call, and an end-of-day client deadline to create context switching while keeping active work central.
