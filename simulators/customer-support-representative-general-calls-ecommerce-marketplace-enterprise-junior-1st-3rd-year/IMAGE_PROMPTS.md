## intro
Filename: public/scenes/intro.png

COPY ONLY THE SUBJECT + STYLE BELOW. DO NOT PASTE THE HEADER OR FILENAME.

Scene-specific subject:
Warm illustrated contact-center desk for a fictional ecommerce marketplace. A headset, white desktop computer (monitor with matching white keyboard), queue timer, and manual binder sit on a tidy desk. The monitor UI is cream-colored and generic with no real company logos or readable private data. Do not include readable customer statements, issue categories, case facts, policy outcomes, answer cues, scores, or anything that tells the player what a caller will say. Do not show a laptop; the computer casing should read as clean white/warm off-white within the muted palette.

Style:
Generate a 16:9 illustration in the same clean editorial 2D style as the attached reference image. Use hand-drawn digital ink linework, flat cel-shaded color, and a very subtle paper-grain finish. The mood is professional, calm, warm, optimistic, and human-centered, like a polished startup pitch deck or refined animation storyboard. Do not make it photorealistic, anime, 3D rendered, watercolor, pixel art, icon-vector, glossy, or painterly.

Use this muted palette as the dominant color system:
- warm cream #EFE8D2
- warm beige/tan #CDBF94
- muted sage green #5F7F62
- dark teal green #3F605C
- muted terracotta red #C75448
- dark charcoal #1E1E1A for outlines instead of pure black

Extra colors are allowed only when the scene genuinely needs them, and they must stay earthy, muted, and compatible with the palette. Avoid neon, saturated primaries, pure black, and large pure-white areas.

Flat color rules:
- Give each object one flat base color and at most one darker shadow shape.
- Do not use gradients, airbrushing, color blending, glow, bloom, lens flare, glossy reflections, ambient occlusion, rim lights, or rendered lighting effects.
- Separate adjacent color areas with charcoal outlines, not gradient transitions.
- A single small flat highlight on glass, screen, or metal is fine; no soft shine.
- Keep screen/interface text generic or unreadable unless exact text is explicitly required by the scene subject.

Perspective and player continuity:
- Use first-person point of view whenever the subject describes the player's desk, screen, hands, or work area.
- Never show the player character's face, head, reflection, portrait, full body, or profile.
- Player hands/forearms may appear if useful. Use a consistent medium warm beige/tan skin tone around #C08F73, with one darker shadow around #8A5F4C.
- Player clothing, if visible, should be plain warm cream #EFE8D2 with no logos, jewelry, watches, rings, bracelets, tattoos, nail polish, long nails, or accessories.

Linework:
- Use thin-to-medium dark charcoal outlines with smooth confident digital ink.
- Make foreground people and key objects slightly heavier than background elements.
- Keep lines clean, with no sketch construction lines, cross-hatching, or mechanical icon strokes.

Shading and texture:
- Use simple cel shading only: flat base color plus one broad shadow shape per object.
- Shadows should be darker versions of the base color, not gray or black.
- Faces should have minimal shading, at most one simple shadow near the jaw, neck, or nose.
- Add only a very subtle paper-grain/printed texture. No watercolor bleed, canvas texture, heavy noise, visible brush strokes, or halftone dots.

Character design, if people appear:
- Semi-realistic adult proportions, slightly simplified, not cartoonish.
- Heads may be only slightly larger than realistic.
- Use almond-shaped eyes, simple dark eyebrows, a small defined nose, and clean lips.
- Hair should be clear flat shapes with one or two internal shadow shapes, not individual-strand realism.
- Hands should be simplified, elegant, clearly drawn, and confidently outlined.
- Skin and clothing must stay flat: one base tone plus at most one shadow shape.

Composition:
- Create clear foreground, midground, and background depth.
- Use a cinematic but natural workplace composition with a clear focal point.
- Foreground objects may crop at the frame edge to create presence.
- Keep the image readable, uncluttered, and grounded in the workplace moment.
- Use flat, naturalistic lighting only; no dramatic noir contrast or spotlight effects.

Match the attached reference image's line weight, flat cel-shading, color saturation, warmth, proportions, and paper texture. Do not copy any specific object, person, pose, or layout from the reference.

Reference image: Match the reference image's linework, cel-shading treatment, color saturation, and character style exactly. But don't copy anything directly from the reference image.
