## Source-Grounded Role Summary
This simulator represents an entry/junior architect at a mid-size residential architecture firm. O*NET describes architects as preparing drawings, coordinating with clients and consultants, applying codes and ordinances, using CAD/BIM tools, inspecting sites, and producing documentation [onet].

The day is a mix of reviewing client needs, picking up senior redlines, updating drawings/models, checking zoning/code constraints, communicating status, and learning how design decisions become coordinated construction documents [reddit:1hh974y][reddit:12eo2up][reddit:17mwbky].

Because this is residential architecture, the student deals with a homeowner remodel/addition brief, existing-condition constraints, setbacks, daylight/privacy concerns, budget sensitivity, permit-set readiness, and site observations [reddit:13yf1i4][reddit:1dzkyeu][reddit:18gmi8a].

## Fictional Workplace
The fictional firm is Harbor & Ash Studio, a 45-person residential architecture practice. The student supports Maple Street Addition, a fictional two-story rear addition and kitchen remodel for homeowners Dana and Luis Moreno.

## Work-Mix Design
Residential junior architecture is primarily digital tool and artifact work: BIM/CAD sheets, redlines, schedules, markup review, permit checklists, emails, meeting notes, and bounded schematic studies [onet][reddit:17zcdma][reddit:14b6qjh]. Cognitive analysis matters because junior staff must understand how a requested change affects code, structure, cost, schedule, and other drawings before editing a model [reddit:15cyslv][reddit:12xw9j5]. Written communication appears throughout the day as intake questions, redline questions, verify coordination summaries, and design notes tied to visible drawing/model decisions [onet][reddit:159xtnc][reddit:12p6n9p]. Spoken communication is focused around team and client clarification [onet][reddit:1alviwh]. Site and field constraints are represented through Riley's verification reply and carried into a Revit-like schematic option study, not as physical site work [onet][reddit:1c0xx7n].
