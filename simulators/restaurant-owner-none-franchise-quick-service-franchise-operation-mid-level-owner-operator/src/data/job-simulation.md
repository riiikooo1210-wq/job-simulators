## Role Reality

A mid-level franchise quick-service restaurant owner-operator is no longer learning every basic routine for the first time. Their day is typical because it blends recurring operating rhythms: reviewing morning KPIs, checking manager notes, approving small labor adjustments, walking the restaurant, inspecting food safety and brand standards, coaching a shift lead, and turning the day's issues into a timed next-day operating board. The role is not only "business strategy" and not only "working the line"; it is making store-level systems repeatable while protecting guest experience, labor cost, food cost, safety, and brand compliance [reddit:alt_nra_labor_costs_2025][reddit:alt_toast_labor_costs][reddit:alt_ifa_franchise_relationship].

In quick service, labor and food cost are constant pressures. A strong owner watches sales, transaction counts, ticket times, waste, callouts, overtime, and schedule coverage together. The correct response is rarely "cut labor" or "add labor" in isolation; the owner has to protect the rush, avoid manager burnout, and ask whether the schedule matches forecasted demand [reddit:alt_nra_sales_food_labor_2025][reddit:alt_toast_food_costs][reddit:alt_qsr_franchisee_economics].

Franchise ownership also means local accountability inside a brand system. The owner has room to coach people, plan local follow-up, and improve execution, but must follow brand standards, supplier rules, operational checklists, food safety expectations, and reporting requirements. The simulator should show brand standards through manager logs, line checks, coaching, and the daily memo rather than as an unusual audit crisis [reddit:alt_ftc_franchise_guide][reddit:alt_ifa_franchise_relationship].

## Fictional Setting

The simulator takes place at **Harbor Bites Express**, a fictional quick-service sandwich and bowls franchise unit in a busy suburban retail plaza. The player is the mid-level owner-operator responsible for one high-volume store. The store has an experienced general manager and a newer shift lead who needs clearer pre-rush ownership [reddit:alt_ifa_franchise_relationship][reddit:alt_ftc_franchise_guide].

The simulated day is a regular Thursday with a forecasted lunch rush, a small but meaningful labor gap, rising waste on grilled chicken, a drive-thru speed problem, and a line-check concern. Nothing is catastrophic. The realism comes from deciding what to inspect, what to delegate, what to document, and how to coach without turning every issue into a personal rescue mission [reddit:alt_qsr_drive_thru_2024][reddit:alt_restaurantowner_checklists][reddit:alt_servsafe_manager].

## Work Mix

The real work mix should be:

- Physical/procedural/tool work: secondary. The owner walks the store and performs line checks, but does not personally execute every crew task [reddit:alt_fda_food_code_pic][reddit:alt_restaurantowner_checklists].
- Digital tool/artifact work: major. KPI dashboards, schedules, manager logs, brand checklists, emails, and timed operating boards drive the day [reddit:alt_square_qsr_operations][reddit:alt_toast_labor_costs].
- Cognitive analysis/decision-making: dominant. The owner chooses what matters now, what can be delegated, and what must be documented [reddit:alt_nra_sales_food_labor_2025][reddit:alt_qsr_franchisee_economics].
- Written documentation/artifact production: major. Store notes, coaching follow-ups, and next-day operating boards make decisions durable [reddit:alt_restaurantowner_checklists][reddit:alt_servsafe_manager].
- Spoken/interpersonal communication: major. Coaching a shift lead and aligning with the GM are recurring owner responsibilities [reddit:alt_restaurantowner_staffing][yt:Rd3DOhCKi5Y].
- Passive monitoring/context switching: secondary. The owner monitors the rush, but the playable scenes should convert monitoring into inspection, prioritization, or communication [reddit:alt_qsr_drive_thru_2024][yt:TCSycJAZVYM].

4. **Line Check Walkthrough**: The player inspects a visible prep line, reads labels and temperatures, identifies issues, and records corrective observations [reddit:alt_fda_food_code_pic][reddit:alt_servsafe_manager].
5. **Jordan Coaching Prep**: The player maps their own line-check notes and earlier KPI plan into Jordan-owned floor actions, then explains which KPI or operating standard each fix supports. The prep is not a script for reciting the KPI dashboard; it translates hot-hold, sanitizer, and drive-thru findings into shift-lead execution [reddit:alt_restaurantowner_staffing][reddit:alt_restaurantowner_checklists].
6. **Coach the Shift Lead**: The player has an in-person voice conversation with Jordan about the missed line-check routine, delegation before the rush, and specific follow-up [reddit:alt_restaurantowner_staffing].
7. **Tomorrow Pre-Rush Operating Board**: The player schedules every required line-check and KPI follow-up into tomorrow's operating timeline, using visible constraints about food safety, staffing, Jordan/Mina verification, rush execution, and post-lunch review [reddit:alt_restaurantowner_checklists].
8. **Assessment Gate**: The day is complete, and the player chooses `View Assessment`.

## Assessment Focus
